Hail, and Well Met

"The winds shift, the rift calls, and the stars stir in their slumber. To walk Ryndor is to walk between worlds—tread lightly, or be swept into the current of forces older than time."

— Ophelia, Praetor Oracle and Leader of the Sev'rinn Rebellion

Welcome, Traveler, to the Weird Lands of Ryndor

Across Ryndor's realms, the scars of old wars linger in stone, steel, and memory, but now, The Weirding spreads through the land, bending reality itself. Nobles and warlords hide ambitions behind silk curtains, while ancient cults stir beneath city foundations, drawn to the shifting astral tides. The land responds to the ambitions of its people — floods, magical storms, and temporal anomalies emerge when the delicate balance is disturbed. Merchants whisper of roads that loop back upon themselves, and scholars dream of cosmic awakenings that are impossible to explain.

In Ryndor, magic, technology, and faith weave together, their fragile interplay destabilized by the flow of Primordial Resonance. This volatile magic, drawn from the tension between the material and astral planes, is now affected by the Weirding, making every spell both a resource and a risk. Sev'rinn practitioners carefully harness this magic, knowing that each use risks fracturing reality further, inviting strange, unintended effects. Entire villages have become trapped in time fractures, while animals and plants mutate unpredictably under the influence of the astral surge.

Technology, once the Ullac Regime's tool to suppress magic, has also been warped by the Weirding. Automaton sentinels malfunction, and old artifacts taken from Nebernoria behave unpredictably. The Rift, initially opened by the reckless ambition of the Regime, is now a gaping wound leaking unstable magic that neither science nor faith can fully control. Praetor Ophelia and Riven Trug, leaders of the Sev'rinn rebellion, rallied against these forces to protect what remains of Ryndor's magic, but the Weirding's spread has complicated their hard-won victory.

Faith, too, is altered by the Weirding. Gods still lend power, but their blessings twist in strange ways, shaped not only by the hearts of their worshippers but by the strange energies that surround them. Magic, science, and faith reflect the intent of those who wield them, but under the influence of the Weirding, even the best intentions can ripple outward unpredictably, bringing unexpected consequences.

Ryndor teeters on the edge of uncontrolled transformation. The Sev'rinn's mastery of elemental essences, the twisted remnants of Ullac technology, and the entities stirring within Nebernoria — all struggle for dominance. Yet, fate in Ryndor is not written in stone; it is woven from the choices of those brave enough to act. In a world where possibility reigns, every step forward shifts the balance — toward salvation or ruin — as The Weirding reshapes the path ahead.

Primordial Resonance

Primordial Resonance is born from the friction between realms, where the fabric of Ryndor brushes against the chaotic expanse of the astral plane. This metaphysical tension creates pockets of raw, unformed energy — an echo of creation itself. At these seams where reality bends, Resonance leaks into the mortal world in unpredictable bursts, as if the two planes cannot help but collide. It is neither stable nor infinite, emerging only where existence trembles — whether from great conflict, moments of heightened emotion, or disruptions in natural law.

The interaction between these planes generates a delicate balance: too much resonance in one place destabilizes the environment, spawning astral storms or temporal anomalies, while too little leaves regions drained of magic entirely. Practitioners who draw on this energy, like the Sev'rinn, tap into the resonance at their own risk, knowing that with every spell they cast, they not only shape reality but also erode the boundary between worlds. As this energy ebbs and flows, so too does the power of those connected to it — leaving Ryndor forever caught between order and chaos, creation and collapse.

Rift to Nebernoria

The rift to Nebernoria is a mystical and mysterious gateway that connects Ryndor to a small part of the astral plane called Nebernoria. The rift is located in the center of Eveltoft, yet it remains a source of wonder and fear for many. The rift is guarded by powerful enchantments and mystical creatures, ensuring that only the most skilled and experienced travelers can pass through it safely. The rift itself is a shimmering, pulsating portal that seems to vibrate with otherworldly energy.

The Rift to Nebernoria was born from the violent friction between the planes, where the boundaries of Ryndor and the astral realm ground against each other until they ripped open a hole in reality itself. This breach occurred in the heart of Eveltoft, unleashing torrents of Primordial Resonance and astral anomalies into the world. The Rift is not just a gateway — it is a wound, ever-expanding and unpredictable, spilling forth both untapped power and unimaginable horrors. Those who venture too close are changed, drawn into its currents or consumed by its chaos.

The Rift's formation altered the balance of magic in Ryndor, creating surges of energy that empowered some while draining others. Attempts by the Ullac Regime to control the Rift through technology and nullification fields failed, for the energy it releases lies beyond mortal comprehension. Now, the Rift looms as both a promise and a threat — offering ancient knowledge and boundless power to those bold enough to claim it, but at the cost of inviting the astral's relentless chaos into the fabric of the mortal world.

Despite the risks, the rift is a source of great significance for the people of Ryndor. It is a gateway to a world of incredible wonder and magic, where the laws of physics and reality as we know them do not apply. It is a place where dreams and nightmares come to life, and where the impossible becomes possible.

The Weirding

The Weirding is a chaotic force born from the friction between the material and astral planes, first unleashed with the appearance of the Rift to Nebernoria. It permeates all aspects of Ryndor, warping reality in both subtle and catastrophic ways. The familiar is transformed into the strange — environments shift, objects behave unpredictably, and creatures mutate into unnatural forms. Time becomes unstable, sometimes freezing or looping inexplicably, and spells can malfunction with dangerous consequences.

No part of society is spared from the Weirding's influence. Magic, technology, and even faith are destabilized, forcing factions to adapt or fall victim to its strange effects. Weird Zones, pockets of extreme distortion, appear unpredictably, altering the landscape and posing new dangers to adventurers and citizens alike. Some see opportunity in the chaos, delving into these anomalies for arcane power or forbidden knowledge, while others work to contain or control it.

As the Weirding spreads, it reshapes Ryndor from a once familiar world into something alien, uncanny, and unstable, forcing those within to rethink survival, progress, and power.

History

The Royal Sev'rinn Bloodline

The Sev'rinns were the rulers of Ryndor for three centuries, since Aldrin Shun became the first Sev'rinn after the Battle of Ardentor.

After years of torment and torture at the claws of the giant Fire Wyrm Ardentor, the armies of Ryndor marched upon his mountain lair and called him out to face them in battle. Thousands of men and women were lost in the Battle of Ardentor, and so it seemed that the nations of Ryndor would also be lost to Ardentor's fires.

Standing alone in an ashen field among the thousands of dead, Aldrin Shun stood and raised his arms aloft as the fearsome Ardentor released a final, massive fireball from his jaws. Instead of being consumed by the flames, Aldrin's skin glowed fiercely, and his hair stood upon its end, as the flames were absorbed into his body.

He swirled the flames around himself, and outstretched an arm to command Ardentor to submit to his will. Ardentor writhed and recoiled from Aldrin's command, and was engulfed in white fire. Ardentor screamed and swiped his massive claws at Aldrin. They fought this way for days, until the dawn of the tenth day when Ardentor finally bowed his head before Aldrin. And Aldrin collapsed in exhaustion upon the giant Fire Wyrm's snout. Ardentor carried him back to his dwelling in the city of Eveltoft, where Aldrin would be declared the Emberclad Sev'rinn.

He became a beloved ruler of Ryndor, and birthed a bloodline of magical progeny that would unite and rule over Ryndor for a prosperous and peaceful age. Aldrin took a wife named Kaida, who was a gifted healer. Some believe her to have possessed some magic of her own, although the truth of her gifts has been lost to time. Kaida and Aldrin had three children: the youngest were twin girls Miyu and Eira, and the eldest a boy named Destin. Miyu and Eira were both gentle and kind, but possessed no magical gift in their blood.

Destin, however, proved to be a powerful wielder of nature magics. He became the first Greenheart Sev'rinn, and contributed much to the taming of wild beasts to use in Eveltoft as work beasts and magical familiars. Destin had two children with his wife Kari: Gyda the elder, and Einar the younger. Kari sadly passed during the birth of their third child, named Sylvi, who did not survive much longer than an hour. So saddened was he by the death of his wife and third child, Destin retreated to the woods with his closest companion, a giant male albino elk called Inge. Ten days passed before he returned, and he was never the same, but far more powerful than before. He started the Hilderturgy, a tradition by which the Elder Sev'rinn would teach and pass down their magical knowledge to their progeny. He taught Gyda and Einar rigorously, and both exhibited magically touched blood. Gyda would soon become the stronger. She possessed power over both water and wind, and became the Tideborn Skycaller Sev'rinn when she was no more than 18.

The following decades were fruitful for the descendants of Aldrin Shun, and many beloved Sev'rinn would bring prosperity and knowledge to all of Ryndor.

Descent into Darkness

Runa, powerful as she was, birthed no magical children of her own. None of her seven progeny could pass the Hilderturgy, and she became desperate to pass her magic to the next heir of Ryndor. She used her ability to commune with the dead to create the dark magic of necromancy, sacrificing the soul of her newest born child in an attempt to create the magical soul of a Sev'rinn. It was thought that this child died in the womb. None but her eldest, Knute, knew the truth.

Runa birthed a grey-skinned child she named Torgar. He passed the Hilderturgy before the age of ten and was a full-blooded Sev'rinn by fifteen — vicious, cruel, and greedy. He lacked the gentleness that was the hallmark of the Sev'rinns before him. He reveled in war and violence, bred the strongest military Eveltoft had ever seen, introduced crippling taxes on surrounding cities, and marched his armies on those who could not pay.

The Torgarian War

A civil war was brewing against Torgar, led by his own brother Knute. Runa attempted to quell the rage in her son Torgar to save Ryndor and Knute. She told him he was birthed from the spirit of her own sacrificed child. Torgar was enraged and heartbroken, and took the life of his mother Runa, while Knute was watching, unknown to both of them. In that moment, Knute called the guerilla army to march against the army of Torgar, and their war tore the city of Eveltoft to shreds. It is said that the war raged for ten years, as the Atmani Sev'rinn Torgar resurrected his armies over and over.

Knute won only by brute force and determination. He marched nearly the entire population of Ryndor to their death to defeat Torgar, and the entire island was devastated for decades following the war. Eveltoft had been razed to the ground, and much of the history of the Sev'rinns was lost. Little is known about what became of the other six children of Runa. It is generally assumed that they were killed by Torgar before the Torgarian war.

The Ullac Regime

After the victory of Knute, he called his regime Ullac, named for law and order. The bylaws of Ryndor were drawn, and the oppressive nature of his regime quickly revealed itself. The ruling class grew more interested in accumulating wealth and power than in serving the people. Taxes were raised to exorbitant levels; minor offenses were punished harshly, with many innocent people imprisoned or executed. Magical creatures and people were hunted down and killed ruthlessly — building the conditions for what came next.

In addition, magical creatures and people were hunted down and killed ruthlessly, leading to a rebellion known as the Sev'rinn Rebellion. The Sev'rinns, who were descendants of the first Sev'rinn, Aldrin Shun, began to revive the old ways, and many people began to turn to them for guidance and protection.

The Sev'rinn Rebellion

Led by the powerful magical Sev'rinn, Praetor Oracle Ophelia, the Sev'rinn Rebellion fought against the Ullac Regime for years. The Sev'rinns, were on the brink of defeat when the rift to Nebernoria suddenly appeared in the heart of Ryndor.

At first, no one knew what to make of the strange portal, but it quickly became apparent that it was the key to the Sev'rinns' salvation. The rift opened up a pathway to Nebernoria, where the Sev'rinns discovered powerful magical artifacts and advanced technology that they used to turn the tide of the war.

The Sev'rinns' forces became augmented with new weapons and armor made from the strange materials found in Nebernoria, and their fighters were trained in powerful spells and magical abilities that gave them a decisive edge in combat. The Ullac Regime was caught off guard by the Sev'rinns' sudden shift in power, and they were unable to withstand the rebels' new arsenal.

In the end, the rift to Nebernoria was a key factor in the Sev'rinns' ultimate victory over the Ullac Regime. The rebels were able to use the resources they found in Nebernoria to improve their lives and the lives of their people, and the rift itself became a symbol of hope and freedom in Ryndor.

The Ullac Regime became increasingly desperate as the rebellion continued to gain momentum. They began to rely more heavily on brutality, which only served to further alienate them from the people of Ryndor. Eventually, their forces were weakened to the point where they could no longer resist the rebels.

In the end, the Sev'rinn Rebellion was victorious, and the Ullac Regime was overthrown. Praetor Oracle Ophelia emerged as a beloved leader and a symbol of hope for the people of Ryndors. She helped to establish a new system of governance that was fair and just for all, and she worked to rebuild the damaged relationships between the various nations that had been torn apart by the conflict.

The Nebernorian Accord

The Rift to Nebernoria brought more than knowledge and danger — it ushered in the beginnings of The Weirding, fundamentally altering the terms of the Nebernorian Accord. As reality itself frayed under the influence of the Rift, the Sev'rinn Council and scholars struggled to maintain control. Strange mutations and anomalies began to emerge among artifacts and explorers alike, complicating efforts to catalog and regulate items retrieved from Nebernoria.

The Keepers of the Rift, once focused solely on controlling passage between realms, were forced to confront escalating Weird Zones forming near the portal — areas that twisted space and time unpredictably, creating challenges for the Keepers and opportunities for rogue factions. The airships of Skyhaven, once reliable vessels for trade and exploration, reported that routes through Weird Zones had begun to change mid-flight, leaving expeditions stranded or lost.

Attempts to negotiate with Nebernorian entities became more erratic as the Weirding intensified, warping communications and intentions. Benevolent spirits became cryptic or volatile, and previously cooperative beings now offered deals laced with strange conditions and lingering curses. The Crystal Scholars scrambled to contain the growing instability, realizing that the very fabric of the Accord could not withstand the shifting nature of the Rift's influence.

Over time, rumors of corruption within the Keepers of the Rift gained traction, as some members grew obsessed with the strange power of the Weirding. Factions seeking to exploit the Rift took advantage of these distractions, smuggling cursed artifacts across Ryndor and spreading further instability. Tensions between the major cities — especially Ban Mynydd and Eveltoft — intensified, with each vying to secure the resources and knowledge the Rift offered, no matter the cost.

Today, the future of the Accord is precarious. With the Weirding now a permanent part of Ryndor, the Rift stands as both a gateway to limitless possibility and a harbinger of chaos. Every expedition, every artifact, and every deal carries risks that can no longer be fully understood. Adventurers must ask themselves not only if the rewards of Nebernoria are worth the danger — but also if the world can survive the consequences of what they bring back.

A Weird Utopia

The Crystal Scholars emerged from the devastation of the War of the Sev'rinns, determined to rebuild society with fairness, justice, and opportunity for all. Under the leadership of the visionary Ophelia, they established a government centered on knowledge, inclusivity, and exploration. Yet their dream of utopia faces an unforeseen challenge — the growing influence of The Weirding.

The Weirding has transformed the Scholars' ambitions, particularly their work around the Rift. While the Rift offers access to unparalleled magical insights and powerful resources, it also leaks chaotic astral energies that twist reality. Artifacts brought back from Nebernoria sometimes carry hidden dangers, causing unexpected mutations or time distortions, forcing the Scholars to adopt stricter protocols. Despite their best efforts, the Weirding has made every expedition a gamble.

The presence of Weird Zones complicates everyday life. Agriculture and trade have been disrupted by spatial anomalies. Education and healthcare — pillars of Ryndor's new order — must now contend with astral plagues and weird afflictions. Magical misfires and technological glitches occur more frequently, but the Sev'rinns work tirelessly to maintain the delicate balance between elements. Though diversity and equality remain core ideals, the Weirding strains even the most robust structures of this utopia.

The citizens of Ryndor know that while The Weirding may change the rules, the dream of a better future still endures — if they are willing to pay the price.

Factions & Powers

Ryndor is shaped by shifting alliances and powerful factions, each vying for control over magic, technology, and political influence — from governments and rogue groups to ideological movements. Each faction presents narrative depth and moral ambiguity, allowing players to ally, infiltrate, or oppose forces throughout their adventures.

👁️
The Ullac Regime
Fallen

The Ullac Regime was built on centralized authoritarian control, with Knute Shun-Rote at its apex as the first Supreme Chancellor. Power was consolidated in a rigid hierarchy — each region governed by appointed bureaucrats and military commanders, all accountable to Eveltoft. Compliance was enforced through surveillance, taxation, and the ruthless suppression of magic.

Though the Regime has fallen, its influence lingers. The most sought-after commodities on the black market are nullification engines and anti-magic technology — highly coveted by factions wishing to suppress or control magic. Former regime operatives, mercenaries, and rogue inventors smuggle these artifacts into cities like Eveltoft and Wyrmbost, where they fetch exorbitant prices.

🔮
The Crystal Scholars
Governing Body

The modern technocratic government of Ryndor, emerged from the ashes of the War of the Sev'rinns and determined to create a society rooted in justice, equality, and knowledge. Led by the visionary Ophelia, they have become a beacon of hope — though their mission has grown more complex with the emergence of the Weirding.

The Rift to Nebernoria serves as both opportunity and threat, channeling strange energies into Ryndor. Scholars stationed around the Rift balance exploration with containment, but the Weirding has impacted council decisions in ways that test their resolve and leadership.

Keepers of the Rift
Secretive Order

A secretive, elite order of scholars, mages, and warriors charged with safeguarding the Rift to Nebernoria. The emergence of the Weirding has altered both their mission and methods. As reality itself frays, the Keepers now face threats not only from Nebernoria's astral entities but from strange anomalies spreading across Ryndor. Weird Zones near the Rift distort time and space, complicating the tasks of maintaining wards and documenting findings.

The Keepers operate from a hidden fortress with increased secrecy, as both the public and rogue factions grow suspicious of their activities. Tensions rise between the Keepers and the Crystal Scholars — some whisper that exposure to the Rift has begun to corrupt the Keepers themselves. Yet they remain loyal to their charge, willing to do whatever it takes to safeguard the Rift, knowing that if the balance tips too far, Ryndor itself could unravel.

Holmean Magical Creature Society
HMCS

Dedicated to the study, preservation, and ethical management of Ryndor's magical creatures, headquartered on the island of Holmea. They collaborate with scholars, Sev'rinns, and drake trainers from Wyrmbost, focusing on endangered species and monitoring the effects of Primordial Resonance.

The Weirding has introduced unforeseen challenges — many creatures now display bizarre mutations, some becoming more aggressive, others developing strange abilities, and certain fauna now resonating with astral energies and warping local ecosystems. The Society also combats the illicit trade of Weird-touched creatures, prized on the black market for their unpredictable properties. Teams of researchers venture into Weird Zones to record changes and retrieve creatures before they are exploited.

Geography & Resources

Ryndor's volcanic origins shape its dynamic landscape — from lava fields and natural hot springs to black sand beaches and snow-capped mountains. The arrival of the Weirding has further altered the land. Entire villages now lie trapped in slow time zones, caught in endless twilight. Holmea's islands float mysteriously above the sea, suspended in the thick mists of a powerful Weird Zone.

Ryndor's waterfalls and rivers, once harnessed reliably for energy, now run unpredictably. Wind-tunnel channels through Eveltoft bring erratic storms, yet the city thrives on its wind and water power. The people adapt — but the Weirding has embedded uncertainty into every aspect of life, as the forces of magic, nature, and technology increasingly warp under this strange new reality.

⛏️ Ban Mynydd
The mining industry flourishes on aluminum and iron veins, but miners report strange seismic anomalies deep underground — entire shafts vanishing, veins shifting position overnight. Some believe astral energies are slowly permeating the earth. Miniature hybrid Fire Wyrms bred in Wyrmbost help navigate treacherous conditions, though they too have begun to show signs of mutation.
🎣 Surreymouth
The southern fishing grounds remain vital, but peculiar mutated sea creatures exhibiting astral properties have begun appearing — some glowing with astral energy, others capable of slipping between dimensions. Fishing remains a dangerous task, though nearly inexhaustible food resources continue to flow from the sea.
🐉 Wyrmbost
Where beasts are bred to work alongside the people. Hybrid Fire Wyrms show signs of new behavior patterns influenced by astral energies. The city's crops struggle as time fractures create erratic growing seasons, making food management increasingly difficult.
🌊 Holmea
Once thought uninhabitable, Holmea now floats ominously above the ocean, surrounded by mist and twisting gravity anomalies. The smaller islands serve as launching points for expeditions into the strange heights of Holmea's floating isle — a place that defies conventional laws of time and space.
Natural Resources

Beyond wind, water, and geothermal energy, Ryndor draws heavily from Nebernoria's rare resources — though they have become more perilous to extract. Nebernite, a prized mineral used to create potent magical artifacts and potions, has grown harder to locate. Astral storms and time fractures make mining operations unpredictable, with some expeditions never returning. Those who do return often find their nebernite deposits altered or corrupted, giving the material strange and volatile new properties.

Neberbloom, a rare plant known for its ability to absorb and store magical energy, has mutated alongside its environment. Some blooms now release waves of raw magic when disturbed, or shift to different planes without warning. Extracting neberbloom requires not just expertise but significant caution — improperly harvested specimens can warp the surrounding landscape or trigger dangerous magical surges.

A Word of Warning

The Weirding's pervasive influence has made Ryndor's natural wealth both a blessing and a curse. While the land still provides, its gifts are increasingly unpredictable, volatile, and dangerous. Every harvest — whether from the sea, the mines, or the Rift — is a gamble. Only those bold enough to adapt can hope to thrive.

Society & Culture

The Society and Culture of Ryndor is as diverse and multifaceted as the land itself, shaped by the interplay of traditions, economy, and competition. Cities like Eveltoft and Wyrmbost boast vibrant festivals and public competitions. Currency flows through the hands of merchants and nobles alike — coins such as the Fimbulmynt and Hrafnmynt forming the lifeblood of trade. Holidays tied to celestial events punctuate the year, while cultural practices reflect regional identities and ancient customs, grounding the people in both heritage and ambition.

Magic & the Sev'rinns

Magic in Ryndor is a deeply ingrained part of culture. Many individuals possess some ability to perform magical feats — magic is respected and revered, but also feared for its potential to cause great harm if wielded irresponsibly. Those born with magical abilities are often seen as chosen by the gods, held in high regard as spiritual leaders or advisers. Yet superstition runs equally deep; some believe those with magical gifts are cursed or possessed.

Sev'rinns hold particular cultural significance — those who experienced The Spark and chose to pursue training in their natural gifts. They are respected members of society, often sought for advice on matters both mundane and mystical. Known for their ability to communicate with spirits of the natural world and commune with ancestors, they are seen as stewards of the land and keepers of ancient wisdom. The training of a Sev'rinn is long and arduous, requiring both natural talent and extensive study of magical theory, and adherence to a strict code of ethics emphasizing the responsible use of their powers.

Religion

Religion plays a vital role in the cultural identity and spiritual lives of Ryndor's people. Faith is deeply respected — seen as a source of both hope and guidance. The gods of Ryndor are believed to be distant and mysterious, influencing the world in subtle ways, lending their power to those whose convictions burn brightest. Yet religion is viewed with a mix of reverence and fear; divine blessings can be fickle, and some believe the gods' attention comes at a price.

The people of Ryndor view faith not as passive belief but as an active relationship with the divine — requiring devotion, rituals, and offerings. Priests and spiritual leaders are highly respected, often serving as advisers to rulers and counselors to the community. Religious festivals and ceremonies are woven into the rhythms of life, marking the changing seasons and the phases of celestial bodies. In some cities, shrines are built atop ancient ley lines or near astral anomalies, believed to channel divine energy more effectively.

The Weirding has further complicated faith. Miracles are no longer always distinguishable from magical anomalies, and divine visions have grown increasingly strange — cryptic dreams, disjointed prophecies, fragmented symbols. Some believe the gods are responding to the disruption of the astral plane; others fear that even the divine is no longer beyond the Weirding's reach. Clergy and prophets struggle to interpret these signs, leading to rifts between religious sects as they debate the meaning behind these new occurrences. Despite this uncertainty, religion remains a powerful force — faith is not simply a way to understand the unknown, but a tool for survival, a source of strength, and for many, a beacon of hope amid the growing chaos.

Social Customs

The people of Ryndor take great pride in their traditions and social customs, viewing them as essential threads in the intricate tapestry of their society. In a land where magic, technology, and faith collide — and where The Weirding constantly disrupts reality — customs serve as anchors of stability, offering comfort and continuity amidst the chaos.

Rite of the Open Hand

In some regions, individuals greet each other by extending the right hand palm-upward — a sign of trust and respect indicating no harmful intent. Refusing to offer an open hand is a grave insult. The Rite also serves as a way to foster peaceful relations between neighboring communities; it is said wars have been resolved through the Rite followed by the Whispered Promise.

The Whispered Promise

When greeting someone for the first time, it is customary in many regions to make a whispered promise — a commitment to aid the other person if they ever need help. The promise is kept secret between the two parties; sharing it with others is a grave offense that can result in social ostracism. In some communities it is seen as a spiritual practice, and those who keep their promises are believed to receive blessings from the gods.

Giving Grove

Many villages and towns maintain communal gardens known as Giving Groves, where members of the community plant and tend crops that are shared freely with anyone in need. Groves are typically protected by local druids, who believe the trees hold a spiritual connection to the land. Seasonal ceremonies of offering are held to renew the grove's energy and ensure its continued vitality for future generations.

Farewell Vigil

After a loved one's death, it is customary to create a small effigy of the deceased from straw, wood, or other natural materials. The effigy is set adrift in a nearby body of water, symbolically releasing the spirit to move on to the afterlife. Family and friends gather to offer prayers and words of farewell as it floats away. Many also create memorial shrines — placed in the family home or a public space — to honor the departed.

Rites of New Life

In many regions of Ryndor, the birth of a new child is celebrated with a special ceremony. Practices often differ based on the race of the child and their family.

Hatching · Drakarim

The clan elder breathes a fiery breath onto the newborn child's forehead, marking them with the clan's symbol. The child is then given a name that represents a virtue the clan values.

Nesting · Leoporin

The family gathers together and creates a special piece of clothing for the baby, embroidered with symbols that represent the child's ancestry and future potential.

Fledging · Aviari

Aviari parents perform a ceremonial flight around their nesting area with their newborn child, introducing them to the spirits of the air and asking for their protection and guidance.

Rooting · Cervar

The family carves a wooden figurine representing the child's spirit animal. This figurine is placed above the child's crib and is believed to provide protection and guidance throughout their life.

Sports & Games

Ryndor’s love of competition runs deep, with sports and games serving as vibrant expressions of the region’s culture, tradition, and unity. From displays of physical prowess to feats of arcane skill and clever strategy, the people of Ryndor embrace sport as a way to push their limits, foster camaraderie, and honor their history. Whether through ancient contests passed down for generations or newer events shaped by the challenges of the modern era, sporting events bring communities together, offering both entertainment and meaning in an ever-changing world.

While The Weirding has reshaped many aspects of Ryndor, its influence on the realm’s official sports remains contested. Weirded objects, creatures, and phenomena are currently banned from formal competitions — such as the Kigan Uldar and Utfordring tournaments — as officials debate how to safely incorporate these unpredictable elements into the rules. For now, competitions remain traditional: focused on skill, strength, and precision. Athletes train hard to master their craft, whether riding the winds of Eveltoft, taming Wyrmbost’s beasts, or navigating the hazards of Utfordring’s obstacle courses.

Kigan Uldar

Kigan Uldar is a popular sport in Ryndor that combines elements of magic and physical prowess. Played on a large open field or arena, two teams of four players each compete to score goals by launching a magical orb — enchanted to emit a fiery glow — through a series of floating rings using only their magical or physical abilities. Players are allowed to use spells and other magical abilities to steal or intercept the orb from their opponents.

The game is played in a fast-paced, high-energy style, with players constantly on the move and employing a wide variety of tactics and strategies. Players can create illusions, conjure barriers, and perform other feats of wizardry to outmaneuver their opponents. Kigan Uldar is highly competitive and physically demanding, requiring speed, agility, quick thinking, and strategic planning. It is popular among both wizards and non-magical folk, and has become a staple of many local festivals and sporting events.

Rules
  • Two teams of four players each. The field is divided into two halves — each team defends their own half and attacks the other’s.
  • At the start of the game, the orb is placed at center field. Players rush forward to take possession. Players may not touch the orb with their hands or any other body part — they must use magical or physical abilities to control it. Non-magic users often use bats, nets, or other tools.
  • Players are not allowed to use spells or abilities that directly harm or injure opponents.
  • Players may not fly or levitate more than three feet off the ground while in possession of the orb.
  • Two halves of 30 minutes each, with a 10-minute break. Most goals wins. A referee oversees play.

Glowstones

A popular tabletop game played across Ryndor, from noble parlors to tavern backrooms. Glowstones is a game of strategy, bluffing, and luck for 2–6 players, played with colored stones, a deck of cards, and dice. Official tournaments ban the Weirded variant, but it thrives in underground circles where unpredictability is part of the fun.

🪨 Materials
36 Glowstones in 6 colors (6 per color: red, blue, green, yellow, white, black) · 54-card Glowstone Deck (Action, Event, and Trap cards) · 6 colored dice (one per color) · Player tokens · Game board with 6 color slots and score tracker · Optional: Betting tokens for tavern variants
⚙️ Setup
Place all 36 glowstones in a draw bag. Each player draws 5 cards from the deck. Lay out the game board with 6 empty color slots. Players roll dice — highest roller goes first, play proceeds clockwise.
Turn Structure
  1. Draw a Glowstone from the bag and place it on the matching color slot. If that slot is full (3 stones), place it on any other open slot.
  2. Play a Card — Action Cards modify stones on the board (swap, steal, add); Trap Cards are played face-down and triggered later; Event Cards introduce effects that affect all players.
  3. Optional: Roll the Glowstone Die matching the color you just placed. Roll a 6: earn bonus points for that color. Roll a 1: the stone becomes unstable and you must remove a stone of the same color from the board.
  4. Bluff or Pass — Players may bluff about having powerful cards. Some cards can only be played if another player challenges them.
Scoring
  • 3 stones of the same color in a row: 5 points
  • 4 stones of the same color in a row: 8 points
  • A set of all 6 colors: 12 points
  • Bonus for rolling a 6 on the Glowstone Die: +3 points
  • At the end of each round, stones reset and a new round begins. Game ends when a player reaches 25 points or all stones are used. Tiebreaker: each tied player draws one stone from the bag; highest color rarity wins.

High Fashion of Eveltoft

Eveltoft, as the heart of Ryndor's politics, trade, and magic, is a city of opulence and expression, where fashion is both a statement of status and an art form. Incorporating pragmatism and layered design, combined with textile elegance and symbolic ornamentation, the high fashion of Eveltoft is as diverse as its inhabitants, with each race contributing unique elements to the city's sartorial identity. Nobles, merchants, and scholars alike use their attire to showcase wealth, lineage, and personal achievement.

🧑 Humans — Elegance through Practicality

Humans in Eveltoft blend utilitarian cuts with ornate embellishments, showcasing their adaptability and influence across Ryndor.

  • Silk-Lined Wool Cloaks — woolen cloaks lined with silk, secured with jade or obsidian brooches; patterns embroidered with family crests and achievements.
  • Layered Robes with Fur Trims — layered robes over high-collared tunics, with wolf or bear fur at cuffs and collars for warmth and sophistication.
  • Statement Sashes and Belts — wide silk sashes embroidered with personal mottos or ancestral stories, paired with leather belts adorned with intricate knotwork buckles.
  • Jewelry with Function — bracelets and rings of bronze and iron with embedded runes for protection; many accessories have hidden pouches for herbs or small items.
  • Structured Caps and Face Coverings — in colder, windier areas: tall, fur-lined caps structured and boxy, with silky face coverings attached by buttons or straps.
🌿 Elves — Ethereal Simplicity with Symbolic Depth

The elves of Eveltoft express their connection to magic and nature through flowing fabrics and symbolic motifs. Their fashion is graceful and minimalist, but laden with meaning.

  • Flowing Silk Tunics and Sleeveless Robes — layered silk garments dyed in soft greens, golds, and blues to mimic water, wind, and sunlight.
  • Embroidered Mythological Creatures — delicate, swirling designs of dragons, birds, and flowers said to offer protection and good fortune.
  • Feathered and Beaded Hair Adornments — feathers, beads, and fine silver chains braided into long locks or worn as delicate headpieces.
  • Barefoot with Ornamentation — many elves go barefoot, using anklets and toe rings adorned with jade or obsidian, signifying connection to the land and sky.
⛏️ Dwarves — Precision and Craftsmanship

The dwarves of Eveltoft take great pride in their metalworking, and their clothing reflects their mastery over materials.

  • Lamellar Vests and Metal-Stitched Cloaks — overlapping metal plates polished to reflect the light; cloaks stitched with thin metal threads or small medallions.
  • Earth-Tone Tunics and Trousers — muted browns, grays, and deep reds, durable but elegant, reflecting connection to the earth.
  • Heavy Jewelry and Beard Beads — thick necklaces, ornate rings, and intricately carved beard beads showing lineage. Gold, silver, and obsidian are favored materials.
  • Boots with Runic Soles — runes engraved on the soles for protection and good fortune; practical for underground travel and stylish for formal events.
✨ Sev'rinn — Elemental Expression

The Sev'rinn use their fashion to reflect their magical affinities and personal connection to elemental forces. Their attire shifts based on which element they most embody.

  • Color-Shifted Fabrics — enchanted fabrics that change colors in response to environmental changes. A water-aligned Sev'rinn's robes might shift from deep blue to shimmering silver in the rain.
  • Flowing Outerwear — loose, billowing robes and capes that respond to the wind, mimicking the natural flow of elements.
  • Masks and Veils — decorative masks or veils symbolizing connection to unseen forces, often adorned with crystals or gemstones that channel magical energy.
  • Bare Feet or Soft-Soled Sandals — earth-aligned Sev'rinn often go barefoot; others wear soft-soled sandals with elemental patterns engraved on the straps.
🌱 Cervar — Nature-Woven Garments

The Cervar, a race connected to nature, weave their clothing directly from plants, moss, and vines, incorporating living elements into their outfits.

  • Living Cloaks and Tunics — garments are partially alive, with flowering plants or glowing fungi woven into the sleeves, symbolizing growth and renewal.
  • Vine-Wrapped Jewelry — bracelets, anklets, and chokers made from vines and roots, decorated with small leaves or blossoms.
  • Natural Color Palette — clothing reflects seasonal colors: greens and browns in spring, fiery reds and oranges in autumn.
  • Symbolic Antlers and Crowns — high-status Cervar wear wooden antler crowns decorated with feathers and stones, marking their connection to nature's wisdom.
🪶 Aviari — Ornate Layers and Flight-Inspired Designs

The Aviari design their clothing to enhance movement and reflect their connection to the sky.

  • Feathered Epaulettes and Capes — epaulettes and capes lined with feathers, creating an ethereal, flowing effect.
  • Lightweight Silk and Leather — leather vests combined with silk undershirts to allow for full mobility.
  • Gliding Sleeves — wide, gliding sleeves that catch the wind as they move, mimicking wings.
  • Intricate Wing Designs on Jewelry — rings and necklaces depicting winged motifs, symbolizing freedom and exploration.
🐇 Leoporin — Playful Patterns and Practicality

The Leoporin balance comfort and charm in their fashion, favoring layered tunics and short capes for agility.

  • Bright Colors and Playful Patterns — vibrant polka dots or geometric designs reflecting their playful nature.
  • Layered Scarves and Sashes — layered scarves wrapped around their necks, secured with decorative pins.
  • Soft Leather Boots — designed for quiet movement, with padded soles.
  • Earrings and Charms — small charms and trinkets worn as earrings or attached to their belts, symbolizing personal milestones or lucky items. It is common to see Leoporin in cities with heavily adorned ears, encrusted with charms and jewels.

Fashion in Eveltoft is not merely about aesthetics — it reflects personal achievement, social status, and magical alignment. The city's citizens use clothing to signal alliances, rank, and ambition, with nobles and scholars wearing inscribed sashes or symbolic accessories that declare their status and affiliations. Special seasonal festivals require elaborate ceremonial outfits, and citizens are judged by the richness of the fabrics and the intricacy of the details in their attire.

Through this blend of resilience and elegance, the people of Eveltoft express their identity and pride. Every thread, gemstone, and accessory tells a story — a story woven into the intricate tapestry that is the culture of Eveltoft.

Abbernusker Lake
⬛ Weird Waters Below the Dragon Mount

At the base of Mt. Bannockwyrm, where shadow and mist coil around jagged cliffs, lies the mysterious Abernnusker Lake — "Obsidian Peak" in the ancient tongue — once rumored to be the birthplace of the legendary dragon Fafnir. Since the Weirding, the lake's frigid black waters have become a wellspring of strange phenomena, bleeding into Nebernoria's astral depths. Strange lights drift beneath the surface, flickering like submerged stars, and fishermen swear they see shimmering cities that dissolve when approached. Those who stare too long into the lake report vivid dream-like visions — fragments of other lives or alternate realities — that linger long after they leave the shore.

Currents beneath the lake flow out of sync with time, dragging objects — and sometimes people — into strange eddies where hours or years can pass in an instant. Fish pulled from the depths are marked with glowing runes or speak in alien tongues before expiring. Locals avoid the waters entirely now, warning that those who swim too close to the center risk becoming lost between worlds, vanishing without a trace.

The creatures haunting the depths have changed. The legendary obsidian-scaled serpent can now split into multiple forms, existing in several places at once. Malevolent nixes have grown aggressive, their faces flickering between multiple forms — often resembling loved ones or lost travelers. Enormous translucent creatures, seemingly half-formed as though caught between two realms, drift through the deep alongside bioluminescent flora that glows in shifting hues of violet and green.

Ardal Plains
🌾 The Sacred Grasslands

The Ardal Plains, stretching between Wyrmbost and Leighmore, are a land steeped in legend and magic. According to ancient lore, the Fen elves once called these plains a sacred sanctuary for the natural world, and travelers passing through would leave offerings for their protection. Even now, some visitors honor this tradition, placing tokens beneath ancient standing stones or whispering thanks into the wind. The lake at the heart of the plains is said to have formed from the tears of a Fen princess, its waters possessing powerful healing properties — though under the Weirding, that healing has grown volatile and unpredictable.

Despite its tranquil appearance, the Ardal Plains bear scars from the Torgarian War. It is said the ghosts of fallen warriors still wander the grasslands, whispering on the wind. Travelers report a chill unease in certain areas, as if the past clings to the landscape like a shadow — voices, faint and distant, carried by the ever-present wind.

The plains host the Annual Kigan Uldar Crown, one of Ryndor's most celebrated sporting events, drawing the best teams from across the land. Though the Weirding has begun to affect other parts of Ryndor, official competitions on Ardal remain untouched — for now. Discussions about allowing Weirded artifacts or mutated creatures into future games stir controversy, but for three days each year, the plains come alive with celebration, competition, and camaraderie that unites Ryndor in rare joy.

Arvanthwaite Woods
🌲 The Danger Wood

The Arvanthwaite Woods, nestled along the northwestern coast of Ryndor, were long regarded as a gift from the elven gods — towering trees, some of the oldest in the land, their roots entwined with ancient magic and forgotten secrets. Since The Weirding, strange energies ripple through the roots and canopies, reshaping the very essence of the forest. Sentient trees, once wise guardians, now stir with unpredictable intent, sometimes attacking intruders with frenzied force even when none is meant. Twisting paths seem to change direction with each step, and travelers risk getting lost in temporal loops, emerging days or years after they entered.

The elves who have vowed to protect this forest find themselves in conflict with its shifting nature. Spirits that once guided travelers have grown hostile. Druids and fey beings still dwell deep within, but even they seem changed — stranger, wilder, and more dangerous. Some whisper the Weirding has awakened ancient beings slumbering beneath the forest floor, beings that predate the elven gods themselves.

Fey creatures, once playful tricksters, have become unpredictable and deadly. Animals exhibit strange mutations — stag-like creatures with glowing antlers, wolves that step between dimensions mid-pursuit. Elves walk a fine line between reverence and wariness, knowing the forest they love is no longer entirely what it once was. Travelers are strongly discouraged from entering Arvanthwaite without a knowledgeable guide, for every step is a journey through mystery and peril where ancient magic and new anomalies blend in equal measure.

Ban Mynydd
⚒️ The Forge of Ryndor

A dense aura of mystery clings to Ban Mynydd, a walled city that thrives amidst darkness and secrecy. Second only to Eveltoft in population, its inhabitants rarely leave — and those who visit often disappear without a trace. The Weirding has taken hold deep within the city's labyrinthine mines, twisting time, space, and reality beneath the surface. Tunnels lead to unpredictable places, sometimes ending abruptly or looping endlessly, and at other times emerging into forgotten realms or alternate timelines. Miners report working for hours only to return to find mere moments have passed on the surface — or, in some cases, years.

The city has become a haven for artificers and arcane scientists seeking to understand and harness the Weirding's effects. Strange minerals extracted from the tunnels create unpredictable results — tools suddenly evaporating, timepieces skipping forward days without warning. Hybrid beasts prowl the tunnels, their forms flickering between physical states; even the fire wyrms bred to assist miners have begun developing additional limbs or glowing with erratic magical energy.

The Custodians of Prosperity — formerly the Empyreal Bank — occupy an imposing fortress behind Ryndor's thickest walls, employing magical wards and nullification fields to safeguard central vaults from temporal distortion. The Frostmark currency remains vulnerable to strange fluctuations — coins aging centuries in moments. No visitors are allowed near the lower vaults, which are rumored to contain not only immense treasures but fractured timelines, where lost miners or forgotten artifacts occasionally emerge from past epochs. Those who seek the hidden knowledge in the deepest tunnels are never quite the same when they emerge — if they emerge at all.

Bennard Peak
⛰️ The Shattered Heights

The towering rock formation known as Bennard Peak — "Firehouse Neck" — stands across from the highest summit of Mt. Bannockwyrm, a monument of stone born, according to legend, from a battle between giants and gods. When the giant chief hurled a colossal boulder at Thor, one strike of his hammer shattered it into a thousand pieces that formed these looming cliffs and the deep canyon below. Since the Weirding, the peak rearranges itself when no one is watching. Strange, flickering apparitions — giants, gods, and warriors frozen in moments from forgotten battles — appear within the mist only to vanish. Time itself falters near the summit, with journeys that should take hours stretching into days, or passing in the blink of an eye.

The canyon beneath is home to gnomes that flicker like candle flames, sprites with translucent wings humming with otherworldly resonance, and trees with twisted branches that seem to reach for travelers. Invisible rivers of whispers flow through the air, and pools of reflective mist show memories that do not belong to the viewer — or sometimes visions of futures they wish not to see.

Bennard Peak remains a sacred site for those seeking divine guidance, though blessings now come tinged with uncertainty. Offerings left at the summit — stones etched with runes, flowers woven with charms, carved wooden figurines — sometimes shift in form or vanish entirely, only to reappear elsewhere. Blessings sometimes twist into curses, and boons often arrive with hidden costs. For those who dare to ascend, the journey offers no guarantees — only the promise of transformation, for better or worse.

Eveltoft
💎 The Crystal Capitol

Following the Sev'rinn Rebellion, Eveltoft rose from the ashes of war to become a beacon of progress and unity — the largest metropolis in Ryndor and the economic heart of the isles. But as it prospered, the early tremors of The Weirding began rippling through its streets. Magic and technology, once harmoniously intertwined, now behave unpredictably — bridges shift, canals flow in strange directions, and buildings subtly twist over time. Parks bloom with strange flora that glow with astral hues, and occasional magical storms disrupt public events.

Citizens walk alongside odd manifestations — astral shadows, rogue enchantments, and strange objects that awaken briefly before falling inert. Weird Zones appear without warning, particularly in poorer districts where magical anomalies intensify existing inequalities. Rivals factions exploit these anomalies to deepen political intrigue. Yet Eveltoft's citizens endure. Festivals continue, trade flourishes, and even amid uncertainty, the people cling to hope — standing proudly at the edge of a reality transformed.

Districts of Eveltoft
🏛️ Central District
Heart of Power and the Rift

At the core of Eveltoft, political authority and commerce converge with the Rift to Nebernoria. Powerful nullification fields — powered by the hydro engines of the Fenchong Falls — are built directly into Eveltoft City Hall, which sits over the roaring waterfall itself. Every so often, fluctuations in flow cause momentary interruptions called Rift Reverberations: objects vanish and reappear, clocks behave erratically, and brief portals to Nebernoria flicker open. Black market rumors abound of engineers willing to disable the nullification fields for the right price.

🪵 Chipping-Gong
Woodlands at the Edge of the Crystal City

Named for a legendary bear once roaming nearby forests, Chipping-Gong is Eveltoft's lumber and woodworking heart. The Weirding has given rise to Weirdwood — timber whose patterns shift like flowing rivers beneath the grain — now a coveted material whose latent magic may alter owners' thoughts or dreams. Buildings have taken on a life of their own, walls tightening against storms, floors softening underfoot. Several unfortunate incidents have involved houses sealing their occupants inside.

🛍️ Dogun
Shopping, Food, Entertainment & Unpredictable Anomalies

Dogun's famous marketplace thrives but strange sights grow common: silk scarves turn to metal in the rain, spices shift taste mid-cooking, and purchased objects vanish by nightfall. Temporary stalls appear overnight, run by beings touched by the astral plane, their wares vanishing with them. The phrase "Dogun will provide" captures both its unpredictability and its potential for surprising good fortune. Lanterns from the annual Festival of Light multiply into hundreds and disappear into strange constellations.

🐟 Fiskmark
Center for Waterfront Fun & Strange Maritime Encounters

Fiskmark's markets and restaurants still thrive, but fishermen return with odd catches — fish that glow faintly in moonlight, crabs with extra limbs, eels that sing eerie melodies. Dishes have become legendary: mirrored cod that displays shifting images of the past as it cooks, ember prawns that warm the eater from inside. Some meals whisper strange thoughts or cause temporary transformations — skin taking on a sea-green hue or hair shifting to translucent strands. The Utfordring league holds competitions in the district's underwater arenas.

⚔️ Heibury
Day Passes Required for Entry

Named for the legendary shield wall once defending the city, Heibury remains Eveltoft's military heart. Training grounds shift underfoot, forcing recruits to adapt mid-spar. Some soldiers develop strange Weirded abilities — reflexes heightened as if guided by unseen hands, weapons glowing with residual energy — but too much exposure leads to fractured memories. The district's blacksmiths create weapons that hum with strange vibrations; some develop minds of their own, refusing to be wielded by those they deem unworthy.

💰 Gullborg
Timeless Splendor Suspended

Once the pinnacle of luxury and opulence, Gullborg District now lies frozen in time, held captive by a slow-time anomaly. From outside, it appears untouched — grand markets bustle, musicians play in courtyards, half-finished meals linger on tables. But for those outside, years have passed without seeing trapped loved ones. Only two individuals have ever escaped since the anomaly began — both returned disoriented, younger than their children trapped outside. Crystal Scholars have yet to find a way to neutralize it.

🌿 Kieferden
A Tranquil Oasis Beneath the Veil of the Weirding

Once a peaceful retreat, Kieferden's lush greenery, spas, and the Ferden Pool — a natural hot spring — now carry a subtle unease. The pool drains one moment and brims over the next; some visitors find immersion warps time, making moments feel like entire days. Massages trigger visions of other lives; herbal teas produce dreams impossible to forget. Rumors persist of a cult devoted to Nebernoria conducting rituals in the district's hidden groves — and the disappearance of several Crystal Scholar investigators investigating those rumors.

Dollow Canyon
🌴 A Verdant Weird

Dollow Canyon, stretching between Mt. Bannockwyrm and Benard Peak, is a lush and humid anomaly in Ryndor's otherwise cool and rugged landscape. Warm air currents funnel into the canyon, making it one of the warmest places in Ryndor, where tropical flora and fauna flourish unchecked. Yet beneath its vibrant beauty, the Weirding makes this jungle treacherous — a living maze where paths open one day and close the next under walls of vines. Plants bloom, wither, and regrow within hours. What seems like a day within the canyon might be only moments outside, and some explorers return with memories of events they cannot explain.

The creatures here are equally unusual. Gigantic predatory plants — flytraps the size of carriages — lurk in the undergrowth, their tendrils exuding hypnotic scent. Insect swarms shimmer with iridescent wings, serpents with bioluminescent scales weave through the branches, and jungle trolls twisted by time and magic can blend into any environment with chameleon-like skin, blinking in and out of reality and seemingly learning from future encounters.

Despite the dangers, rare herbs and plants with powerful medicinal properties grow only here — flowers that glow under moonlight, mosses that heal wounds instantly. Potions brewed from Dollow's plants grant immense power or strange dreams, though prolonged use blurs the lines between reality and hallucination. The environment shifts so frequently it is almost impossible to map: new rivers appear overnight, waterfalls cascade from seemingly nowhere. The jungle seems alive, responding to the thoughts of those within it — paths opening for those with pure intentions, closing abruptly for those weighed down by greed or fear.

Hurstwold Forest
🍄 Deep, Dark Mysteries in the Trees

Hurstwold Forest seems only half-tethered to the material world. Trails appear and disappear at will, leading travelers in circles for days or dropping them into places they never intended. Dense trees loom overhead, their trunks seemingly breathing with strange patterns of light and shadow. The air is heavy with cool, shifting mist that dampens both sound and senses, making it impossible to know how far one has traveled — or whether they are still within the same moment they began. Moss glows faintly underfoot, shifting colors in response to emotions and thoughts, as though the ground itself is alive and aware.

The magical creatures that once called Hurstwold home — faeries, sprites, and unicorns — have been irrevocably altered. Some flicker in and out of existence as though trapped between realities. Innerstifla Sound

The Innerstifla Sound, separating Leighmore from the ancient Hurstwold Forest and the coastal town of Norwycke, was named for the ice that once reliably coated its surface in winter. Ice forms overnight even in summer, only to crack and melt within moments. Phantom storms appear and vanish within minutes, leaving behind strange debris — barnacled artifacts and bones not of this world. Entire stretches of the sound become mirrors of other places or timelines, revealing alternate versions of the ships that sail them.

The Crystal Scholars suspect a submerged rift below the sound — a secondary faultline leading to Nebernoria. Despite immense danger, the sound remains an essential trade route, with Fishermen's Guild crossings organized regularly. The Keepers of the Rift maintain a watchful presence along its shores, fearing the influence could spread further — yet even they admit the waters are unpredictable, and that one day something too large to contain may rise from its depths.

Inverlinn Coast

Adventurers seeking glory are drawn to the coast hoping to discover lost relics or new forms of magic — many return only with fragmented memories, or not at all. Isles of Holmea

Norwycke

The Hurstwold Forest has changed dramatically at Norwycke's edge. Trails flicker in and out of existence, and travelers see themselves walking ahead or behind on parallel paths. Pockets of altered time flow within the forest, causing some adventurers to emerge years later believing only hours had passed. Others reappear strangely changed — speaking languages no one recognizes, carrying artifacts from places existing only in dreams.

Norwycke is a paradox: a quiet town no longer sitting fully within one reality, drifting at the edges of time and space. Some claim the town itself has grown sentient, deciding who may come and go. Those who flee other Weirded zones often seek refuge here — but many who stay too long find it increasingly difficult to leave. Surreymouth

Surreymouth, once a thriving trade hub on the southern coast of Ryndor, now stands transformed by The Weirding's unpredictable influence. Water travel is still preferred over land routes, as the terrain warps unpredictably, storms appear without warning, and strange beings emerge from places where they should not exist. The harbor is a kaleidoscope of strange vessels — some ordinary, others twisted by the Rift's influence.

The Surreymouth Port Authority struggles to maintain its grip. Entire manifests rewrite themselves during inspections, customs officials navigate goods that vanish only to reappear days later in another district, and the Coast Guard encounters phantom ships caught in time loops. Smuggling rings use strange weather patterns to move illicit goods, including weirded artifacts highly sought in black markets. The organized crime families of Surreymouth thrive under this new uncertainty, offering protection and predictability to those willing to pay.

The Deep League of Surreymouth, home to the aquatic sport of Utfordring, now finds itself in uncharted waters. In Surreymouth, the line between success and ruin is as thin as a whisper — every venture a bet against the capricious nature of the Weirding itself.

Wenport

The old lighthouse, long a beacon of safety for sailors, now emits occasional pulses of light that some claim reveal glimpses of distant shores — or forgotten memories. Ships sometimes arrive with no sign of their crews, decks covered in salt-encrusted barnacles, though the journey had only been a few days.

Strange trinkets find their way into shipments: barnacle-covered coins, bits of driftwood carved with incomprehensible symbols. The annual Seabreeze Festival has taken on new meaning. Some say it appeases whatever stirs in the water — fishing competitions, boat races, and seafood tastings still draw crowds, but a new event has emerged: The Lantern Drift, where townspeople release glowing lanterns into the harbor hoping to guide lost sailors or appease restless spirits. The maritime museum now hosts strange artifacts found along the shore — driftwood with unfamiliar carvings, barnacle-crusted jewelry, and scraps of sailcloth from ships that never docked. The people of Wenport endure, determined to face the unknown together, just as they've weathered every storm before.

Wyrmbost
🐲 Home of the Dragon Farms

Wyrmbost has always been modest compared to the bustling centers of Eveltoft and Surreymouth, but its role within Ryndor is indispensable. Crops sometimes shift color mid-season, growing with unsettling speed or twisting into unfamiliar forms. The people of Wyrmbost remain close-knit and grounded, finding strength in their traditions and relationships with the land and their beasts. The main square, dominated by a statue of Destin, stands as a reminder of the town's proud history. An ancient stone bridge, still in use today, symbolizes Wyrmbost's ability to weather change. The saying passes through every family: "Even the land must learn to dream differently now." Some breeders have begun experimenting with Weirded dragons, exploring their unique abilities. A few visionaries believe the Weirding is not a curse but a gift waiting to be understood — and in Wyrmbost, where the bond between person and dragon remains unbreakable, they may be right.

🪓 Barbarian

Barbarians in Ryndor are more than warriors—they are primal forces bound to the land, embodying the wild, unyielding spirit of the world itself. In a land where nature's fury shapes the very culture, barbarians are revered and feared in equal measure. They are living tempests, forces of pure instinct and will, drawn from the deepest roots of Ryndor's wilds and mystical forces. Whether shaped by the crashing waves along Surreymouth's harbors, the raging fires within Ban Mynydd's forges, or the howling winds of the Bannockwyrm peaks, Barbarians in Ryndor are unpredictable forces, unleashed to conquer or protect.

Origins of the Barbarian Spirit

Every barbarian in Ryndor carries with them a story of survival. Many have grown up in the untamed expanses of Hurstwold Forest or the dangerous Dollow Canyon, where the land itself tests one's mettle daily. Others trace their rage to ancient ancestors who defied oppression, especially those who fought in the Sev'rinn Rebellion, their fury echoing through generations as a legacy of resistance. Some barbarians are wanderers, shaped by a nomadic existence and a deep connection to the natural and spiritual forces of the world. While they may come from vastly different regions and backgrounds, all barbarians share an affinity for raw emotion—a connection to the world not through logic or study, but through feeling, instinct, and rage.

Barbarians and the Weirding

Barbarians have a complex relationship with the Weirding, the strange and unpredictable phenomenon born from the friction between Ryndor and Nebernoria. For some, the Weirding is an unwelcome curse that disrupts their control, as time shifts unpredictably or landscapes dissolve before their eyes. For others, it is a gift—another chaotic force to harness and channel through their rage. Stories tell of barbarians who have wandered into Weird Zones and returned altered, their very essence marked by the phenomena—time-scarred warriors who fight with unnatural precision or power that shifts like a storm on the horizon.

Some clans, especially those in remote areas, revere the Weirding as an extension of the natural world, seeing it as proof that the land is alive and evolving. These barbarians often experiment with strange relics and artifacts, willing to risk corruption for a taste of the Weirding's potential. Others serve as protectors against its dangers, confronting beasts twisted by the Weirding and guarding settlements near unstable zones.

The Role of Barbarians in Ryndor's Society

Barbarians are often outsiders to the cities and organized powers of Ryndor. Many hail from regions where Kigan Uldar and Utfordring competitions are more than sport—they are tests of survival, where strength and cunning are honored above all else. In places like Norwycke or the Ardal Plains, a barbarian's rage is seen as a tool of justice, a way to protect the weak and mete out vengeance against oppressors.

Some barbarian clans serve as guardians of sacred places, like the ancient groves of Hurstwold or the treacherous jungle depths of Dollow Canyon. Others act as mercenaries or bounty hunters, employed by guilds in Surreymouth or Ban Mynydd to hunt down dangerous prey. While the cities of Ryndor may view barbarians with caution, they are also respected for their unwavering resolve, often called upon in times of crisis when brute force is needed to protect against invading forces or unnatural threats.

Paths Unique to Ryndor

Barbarians in Ryndor are shaped not only by their innate rage but by the worlds they inhabit. Many follow primal paths unique to their regions, their powers reflecting the environment or forces that shaped them. Whether they embody the unstoppable fury of nature, the essence of ancient storms, or even tap into the chaos of the Weirding, each barbarian is a reflection of the wild, untamed heart of Ryndor. These warriors stand apart from organized military orders, refusing the constraints of laws or rulers. Instead, they act with the freedom of those who answer only to their instincts, embodying the principle that power lies in action, not in words.

Path of the Howling Tempest

Barbarians who follow the Path of the Howling Tempest draw their rage from the elemental winds and storms that surge across the jagged terrain of Ryndor. These warriors embody the unpredictable fury of the storm—violent, swift, and relentless. In their rage, they move like a tempest, their attacks accompanied by bursts of wind and thunder. The Howling Tempest barbarians harness the essence of the storm not only for destruction but to protect allies caught in nature's fury. Their presence on the battlefield is heralded by gales that buffet their foes and empower their comrades.

Every bard in Ryndor embodies the idea that knowledge, creativity, and music are forces capable of shaping reality. In the great halls of Eveltoft, bards serve as advisers, spinning intricate webs of words to influence political decisions and manipulate shifting alliances. In the black markets of Surreymouth, clever bards weave tales of fortune and caution to sway deals in their favor. Some serve as emissaries, traveling between the major cities—Norwycke, Ban Mynydd, and Wyrmbost—using their charisma and wit to build fragile peace between factions.

With the emergence of The Weirding, bards have taken on new responsibilities—and dangers. Strange melodies and half-forgotten songs now echo in unpredictable patterns, seemingly drawn from the rift to Nebernoria. Some bards have begun to explore these eerie harmonies, crafting new spells and performances to harness the Weirding's unstable energies. Yet the cost is high. Bards who channel the Weirding too deeply often return altered or afflicted, with new songs in their repertoire—along with haunting memories they can never quite escape.

Traditions of Performance and Lore

Not all bards seek out the strange energies of the Weirding. Many uphold the ancient tradition of the Skalds, who once traveled Ryndor, chronicling battles and memorializing heroes through epic poems and ballads. These Skaldic bards find power in recalling Ryndor's oldest legends—some believe the act of retelling these stories strengthens the land itself, preventing further destabilization from the Weirding.

Kigan Uldar and Utfordring games have also created new paths for bards, as they turn arenas into theaters of spectacle and sport. Arena Bards are celebrated as much for their charisma as for their spellwork, their stirring songs becoming an integral part of the competitive atmosphere. Victory in the Deep League is marked by both physical prowess and the brilliance of the accompanying performance, creating heroes who are immortalized in song across the land.

The Bardic Styles of Ryndor's Races

  • Humans. Bards among the human population blend diverse influences, mastering both ancient storytelling techniques and cutting-edge performance magic. Human bards often serve as political advisers or wandering troubadours, using their versatility to adapt to any audience.
  • Elves. Elven bards focus on elegant, intricate melodies that weave illusion and charm into their performances. Their songs are often seen as subtle spells, with magic hidden in every harmony and poetic verse. Elves are particularly skilled at manipulating Primordial Resonance, allowing their music to harmonize with the natural world.
  • Dwarves. Dwarven bards prefer epic tales accompanied by booming chants and percussion. Their performances honor ancestral heroes, and their songs contain powerful evocations that inspire allies to stand firm even in the direst of battles.
  • Sev'rinn. Sev'rinn bards explore the intersections between magic and emotion, focusing on the essence of memory and transformation. Their songs can evoke lost dreams or reshape a listener's perspective, reflecting their deep connection to the ever-changing forces of Ryndor.
  • Cervar. Cervar bards emphasize movement and dance, using their performances to mimic the fluidity of nature. Their songs are often playful, imitating the sounds of forests and rivers, yet layered with hidden meanings and insights.

Weirding-Touched Bards

Some bards in Ryndor have begun to explore The Weirding's strange echoes, discovering how unstable resonance patterns can create powerful, albeit unpredictable, magic. These Weirding-Touched bards are considered both geniuses and outcasts—capable of shaping reality with unsettling melodies, but often at the cost of their own sanity. Instruments exposed to the Weirding hum with chaotic energy, shifting in shape or producing dissonant, otherworldly tones.

A new subset of spells has emerged among these bards, tapping into the essence of change, memory, and temporal distortion. Some Weirding-Touched bards have even learned to weave their spells into conversations, allowing them to alter time and perception during moments of diplomacy, interrogation, or combat. However, using such magic is fraught with risk, and those who delve too deeply into the Weirding are often left scarred by the experience.

Clerics in Ryndor are powerful conduits of divine will, individuals who wield the blessings of gods and cosmic forces to shape the world around them. The Nature of Divinity in Ryndor

The people of Ryndor worship a wide array of gods, spirits, and cosmic entities—some ancient, others born from the chaos of the Weirding itself. Faith in Ryndor isn't always about reverence alone; it's about alignment with a greater purpose. Clerics see their power as both a gift and a responsibility, their actions rippling outward in ways that alter reality itself. As guardians of both the physical world and spiritual realms, clerics serve on the front lines against both mundane threats and supernatural phenomena.

Some clerics strive to restore order and stabilize regions affected by the Weirding, while others delve into its chaotic potential, believing disruption is the first step toward creation. Regardless of their path, every cleric must tread carefully, as even divine power can falter or mutate in ways that defy understanding.

Faith and the Weirding

The Weirding has added new challenges—and opportunities—for clerics. Some regions of Ryndor have become spiritually fractured, and divine magic behaves unpredictably within the Weird Zones. In some cases, prayers are answered with strange, unintended consequences, or visions come from long-forgotten gods who stir in the rift between planes. Clerics attuned to these disturbances may develop strange new abilities—or encounter beings who demand dangerous bargains in exchange for power.

For many clerics, maintaining faith has become a daily struggle, as the cosmic forces they serve are no longer as stable or predictable as they once seemed. The faithful must learn to adapt, becoming both guides and protectors in a world that now teeters between planes of existence.

Role of Clerics in Ryndor's Society

Clerics hold unique positions of influence in Ryndor. In cities like Eveltoft, they serve as advisors, healers, and judges, helping to resolve disputes and maintain balance within society. Rural communities often rely on clerics as spiritual leaders and protectors, offering guidance through both divine teachings and practical magic. However, not all clerics are celebrated—distrust and superstition linger in areas where the Weirding has caused divine magic to behave unpredictably, leaving some wary of the clergy's intentions.

In some regions, particularly those touched by the Sev'rinn influence, clerics combine their religious teachings with elemental magic to enhance their abilities, crafting strange rituals that call upon both divine and natural forces. Others ally themselves with powerful city-states or guilds, wielding their magic in exchange for influence, wealth, or knowledge.

Clerics and the Weirding Conflict

Clerics play a pivotal role in the battle against the Weirding. In regions where the Weird Zones blur the lines between planes, clerics must use their magic to stabilize the area—or risk becoming part of the chaos themselves. Some clerics form alliances with scholars and wizards to study the effects of the Weirding, hoping to unlock its mysteries and tame its power. Not all clerics agree on how to confront the Weirding. Some see it as a test of faith, sent by the gods to challenge Ryndor's people. Others believe the Weirding is evidence of divine imbalance, requiring new rituals and practices to restore cosmic harmony.

Druids in Ryndor are the guardians of nature, but in a world shaped by the Weirding, they are more than just protectors of forests and fields—they are intermediaries between the natural and the unnatural. The strange influences that have emerged across the land challenge the very balance they have sworn to protect. As beings of both tradition and transformation, druids must adapt to the ever-changing nature of Ryndor. The Role of Druids in Ryndor

The druidic tradition in Ryndor stretches back to ancient times, predating the rise and fall of empires. Many druids act as shamans, guides, and advisors, respected for their wisdom and understanding of nature's cycles. However, the spread of the Weirding has unsettled them. Some druids resist these changes, trying to maintain the old ways, while others embrace the strangeness as part of nature's evolution. Druids in Ryndor often serve as watchers of boundaries: between life and death, order and chaos, and material and astral realms. Druidic Traditions in Ryndor

  • Ancient Guardians. Some druids uphold the oldest traditions, protecting ancient forests like Hurstwold or Arvanthwaite Woods. They are wary of the Weirding and seek to isolate these places from its influence.
  • Wayfarers and Guides. These druids walk the wilds of Ryndor, helping travelers survive treacherous terrain and avoid strange phenomena. They understand that balance requires movement and change.
  • Weird-Touched Mystics. A growing number of druids now study the Weirding's effects on natural phenomena. They explore strange groves and mutated ecosystems, seeking ways to harness the unpredictable forces of this new world.

Druids in Daily Life

Druids rarely reside within cities, preferring to live on the fringes of civilization or within natural sanctuaries. In Eveltoft, druids play a quieter but essential role, ensuring that the city's interaction with the Weirding is carefully monitored. Some act as emissaries between the Crystal Scholars and the wilderness beyond, negotiating for harmony in a world shifting between order and chaos.

Fighters are the steadfast warriors of Ryndor, trained in the art of combat from a young age. Fighters in Ryndor are as diverse as the regions they come from, with their techniques shaped by the land, sky, and strange forces like The Weirding.

Warriors of Every Kind

Fighters in Ryndor adopt countless roles, from disciplined knights sworn to protect the realm, to bold adventurers carving their path through the unknown. Some serve as mercenaries or soldiers, while others become champions in the Kigan Uldar Games, where the most daring and skilled combatants compete for glory. Many Fighters also dedicate themselves to causes—whether defending communities from Weirded creatures, mastering ancient martial traditions, or unearthing the lost combat knowledge of Nebernoria.

The Weirding and Fighters

The Weirding has drastically altered both the landscape of Ryndor and the techniques of combat itself. Some Fighters have adapted to these strange changes, incorporating elements of Weirded environments into their tactics. Warriors who brave Weird Zones learn to expect the unexpected, mastering fast reactions and strange combat strategies to outlast supernatural effects. However, others are more cautious, relying on tradition and discipline to remain grounded amidst the chaos. In this new age, even the most skilled Fighters must stay alert—every battlefield may shift beneath their feet, and every opponent could harbor unforeseen dangers.

Role in the World

Fighters play an essential role in Ryndor's society. In Eveltoft, they serve as guards, soldiers, and gladiators in the city's many competitions. Those Fighters who venture into the Weirded wilderness face immense danger but often return with stories and treasures beyond imagining. Fighters are also key figures in the Kigan Uldar and Utfordring competitions—their mastery of weaponry and tactics makes them crowd favorites, and many seek fame and fortune through victory in these grand events.

Fighters in Society

Each culture within Ryndor views Fighters differently. Some regions treat them as noble defenders, while others see them as mere mercenaries-for-hire. In Eveltoft, Fighters trained in the Heibury District are respected for their discipline, while the warriors of Ban Mynydd carry the weight of tradition. Many rural Fighters serve as both hunters and defenders, protecting their homes from wild beasts and strange threats. In Ryndor's ever-shifting power dynamics, Fighters may align with different factions—mercenary guilds, royal armies, or shadowy organizations. Regardless of their allegiance, Fighters are always at the forefront of conflict, ready to face whatever challenges the world throws their way.

🏹 Ranger

In the vast and unpredictable landscape of Ryndor, Rangers stand as masters of survival, navigation, and reconnaissance. They are adept at thriving in both the untamed wilderness and urban sprawl, moving with quiet precision through dense forests, shifting sand dunes, and bustling markets. Rangers in Ryndor blend ancient traditions with new techniques—many learn the ways of both elemental resonance and primal intuition, making them uniquely attuned to the Weirding that affects the land.

Themes of the Ranger Class in Ryndor

  • Guardians of the Frontier. Rangers serve as scouts, trackers, and protectors, often patrolling dangerous borders or maintaining the safety of remote settlements. Whether guarding the trade routes of Surreymouth or the dense Hurstwold Forest, they are always on the move.
  • The Resonance of Nature. Many Rangers develop a strange attunement to the elements of Ryndor. They can sense shifts in weather patterns, the strange pulses of Weirded zones, and track the subtle flow of life in regions affected by elemental resonance.
  • Wanderers and Guides. Rangers rarely stay in one place for long. Their journeys through the wildlands or the decaying remnants of ancient cities make them invaluable guides, often employed by scholars, merchants, and adventurers alike.

Subcultures and Regional Roles

  • The Greenhearts of Hurstwold. These Rangers are deeply in tune with nature's primal forces, making them exceptional trackers and guardians of wildlife.
  • Voidstriders of Ban Mynydd. Masters of stealth and exploration, these Rangers navigate dangerous mines, Weird zones, and dimensional rifts to scout for threats and ancient artifacts.
  • Seawatchers of Surreymouth. Coastal Rangers who patrol the shores and trade routes, specializing in managing maritime dangers like storms and sea monsters.

The Ranger and the Weirding

The Weirding presents a unique challenge—and opportunity—for Rangers. Some develop strange abilities from exposure to Weirded zones, granting them limited powers of foresight or the ability to sense hidden pathways. Others choose to guard the land from the harmful influence of the Weirding, hunting down dangerous anomalies that threaten Ryndor's fragile balance. Archetypes unique to Ryndor include Skyward Stalkers (attuned to the skies, riding giant birds), Echo Wardens (tracking resonant anomalies, wielding elemental forces in subtle ways), and Lantern Guides (operating in Eveltoft, using enchanted lanterns to reveal hidden pathways and creatures).

Rangers in Ryndor embody resilience, adaptability, and loyalty to their chosen path. Whether traversing ancient ruins or standing guard against unseen forces, they are the first line of defense—and often the last hope—against the ever-shifting dangers of the Weirded Lands.

Arcane Mechanist

The Arcane Mechanist is a Ranger who merges nature's mastery with the technological ingenuity of Ryndor. These Rangers are experts in using and maintaining the realm's advanced tools, airships, and mechanical companions. Rather than drawing power solely from primal forces, the Arcane Mechanist taps into technology to enhance their abilities, making them invaluable guides, scouts, and saboteurs. Whether calibrating intricate gadgets or adapting machinery to strange environments, these Rangers are masters of innovation and resourcefulness.

  • Tinker's Tools & Tech Sense (3rd level). Gain proficiency with Tinker's Tools. As an action, detect magical or technological traps, contraptions, and energy sources within 60 feet. You can also identify how to disable or manipulate them with a successful INT (Investigation) check.
  • Mechanical Companion (3rd level). Build a mechanical companion—a small automaton (bird, spider, or fox). It acts on your turn and can use the Help action or deliver a simple ranged attack (1d6 piercing damage, range 30/60 ft). If destroyed, you can repair it over a short or long rest.
  • Deployable Devices (7th level). Prepare two devices each day, choosing from: Smoke Bomb (10-foot-radius cloud of smoke for 1 minute, heavily obscured), Shock Dart (2d8 lightning damage on a hit + can't take reactions until start of its next turn), or Spring Trap (hidden mechanical trap, 2d6 piercing damage + restrained on trigger, STR save to escape).
  • Overclock (11th level). Once per short or long rest, activate Overclock Mode for 1 minute: your mechanical companion gains an additional action per turn and advantage on all attack rolls; you can use two deployable devices as a bonus action during Overclock.
  • Arcane Infusion (15th level). Store a spell of 3rd level or lower into your mechanical companion or any deployable device. Once the spell is triggered, it takes effect. Recharges on a long rest.
🗡️ Rogue

In Ryndor, Rogues are not merely thieves and tricksters; they are adaptive survivors, master infiltrators, and cunning strategists. The evolving complexities of the Weirding have only sharpened their talents. Rogues serve as spies, treasure hunters, black market operators, and saboteurs, blending stealth, wit, and an innate ability to manipulate environments—whether in a bustling city or Weirded wilderness. They are the ones who find opportunities where others see barriers, navigating dangerous trade routes, crime networks, and political intrigue with unmatched expertise.

Masters of Deception and Strategy

Rogues thrive within Ryndor's labyrinthine politics and dangerous trade networks. They may operate behind the scenes in Surreymouth's black market, gather intelligence on behalf of Eveltoft's Crystal Scholars, or serve as elite operatives hired by the Custodians of Prosperity. From the back alleys of Gullborg to the untamed depths of the Weirded Hurstwold Forest, these agents are everywhere, unseen but always watching. Their subtle manipulation of events makes them invaluable, though many question their loyalty.

Stealth in the Weirded Lands

The unpredictable forces of the Weirding provide both challenge and opportunity for Rogues. With a knack for improvisation, they exploit the strange physics and hidden pathways caused by Weird Zones. Many Rogues have perfected the art of blending their skills with the Weirding's unpredictable effects, mastering the ability to vanish from sight or slip through dimensional gaps. Whether avoiding the scrutiny of the Keepers of the Rift, smuggling Weirded artifacts from Nebernoria, or deceiving magical creatures drawn to the Rift's strange energy, Rogues in Ryndor have learned to thrive in a world where nothing is certain, and everything can be used to their advantage.

Subtle Yet Deadly

The Rogue's toolkit includes stealth, lockpicking, sleight of hand, and the ability to read people and situations at a glance. They rely on finesse over brute strength, using poison-tipped blades, hidden weapons, and traps. In the Weirded Lands, Rogues favor mundane objects imbued with strange quirks—items whose effects are unpredictable but highly effective in the right hands. The Rogue class embodies the unpredictable nature of Ryndor itself—always adaptable, always two steps ahead. These agents, thieves, and tricksters may serve noble or selfish causes, but in a world ruled by uncertainty, they are the ones best equipped to thrive.

Riftstalker

Riftstalkers are Rogues who have mastered the art of moving between dimensions, exploiting strange anomalies and spatial quirks caused by the Weirding. These nimble operatives thrive in areas destabilized by the Rift to Nebernoria, slipping through folds in space, vanishing at a moment's notice, and reappearing where they are least expected. Whether acting as smugglers, spies, or covert agents, Riftstalkers bend space to their advantage, becoming elusive hunters in a world filled with instability.

  • Dimensional Step (3rd level). As a bonus action, momentarily shift between spaces. Teleport up to 15 feet to an unoccupied space you can see. Uses equal to proficiency bonus; recharges on a long rest.
  • Anomaly Sense (9th level). You develop an instinct for detecting disturbances in space. Advantage on Perception (WIS) and Investigation (INT) checks to detect hidden portals, dimensional anomalies, or creatures affected by spatial magic.
  • Rift Ambush (13th level). After using your Dimensional Step, you can add your Sneak Attack damage even if you don't meet the usual conditions, as long as you appear adjacent to your target.
  • Fractured Self (17th level). As an action, summon alternate versions of yourself from nearby timelines. Create 1d4 illusory duplicates that last for 1 minute. These duplicates mimic your movements, imposing disadvantage on all attack rolls targeting you.
✨ Sorcerer · Warlock · Wizard

Arcane specialists of all three traditions operate throughout Ryndor, each shaped by the land's unique pressures. Sorcerers in Ryndor often trace their power to draconic bloodlines (particularly common among Drakarim), elemental resonance, or exposure to Weirded energy. Their magic surges with the same unpredictability as the land itself. Warlocks find no shortage of patrons near the Rift to Nebernoria—strange entities linger at the boundary between worlds, eager to bargain with those willing to listen. Wizards cluster in Eveltoft's Crystal Scholar institutions and aboard research airships, systematically cataloguing and containing the Weirding's effects. All three classes use standard PHB subclasses, though the Weirding's influence shapes how arcane magic manifests—expect the occasional unpredictable result in Weird Zones.

🔥 Sev'rinn Unique to Ryndor

The Sev'rinn are not mere spellcasters; they are living conduits of elemental power, capable of summoning the primal forces of nature in the heat of battle. Elemental mastery is woven into their identity—whether they conjure fire to engulf their weapons, invoke gusts of wind to evade attacks, or summon stone armor mid-battle. A Sev'rinn commands elemental magic with instinct rather than study, adapting moment by moment to the demands of combat. However, every spark of power drawn from the elements risks opening a doorway to the Weirding, a chaotic influence that changes the battlefield in unpredictable ways.

Sev'rinn use Elemental Techniques—swift invocations and battle actions powered by Elemental Charges, rather than traditional spell slots. These warriors blur the line between martial combatants and arcane masters, making them a force to be reckoned with on the battlefield.
